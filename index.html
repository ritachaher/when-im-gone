When someone dies, as hard as the grief — is not knowing where anything is

Most families spend weeks piecing together paperwork. It doesn't have to be that way.

"Where's the will?"

Your family shouldn't have to hunt for a solicitor's name during the worst week of their lives.

"Which accounts exist?"

Bank accounts, pensions, insurance policies — most families miss at least one. Some never find them.

"What did they want?"

Burial or cremation? Which music? Who to call first? Don't leave them guessing when it matters most.
